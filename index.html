Yakelyn R. Jauregui

Affiliation

I am a graduate researcher at the University of Washington in the department of Atmospheric Science. I started the program in 2017 and I work under the supervision of Prof. Shuyi Chen.

I have a background in Tropical Meteorology and Physical Oceanography. I use both in-situ and satellite observations in addition to model simulations to understand the atmopshere-ocean coupled interaction in the tropical Pacific Ocean. For more, take a look at my curriculum vitae or publications.

My curiosity and deep desire to understand the atmosphere and ocean interaction stems from the enourmous world-wide societal impact of the most important coupled tropical phenomena, The Madden-Julian Oscillation (MJO) and El Niño Southern Oscillation (ENSO). I am from Peru, an agricultural country, and our society depends on seasonal products harvested in the country-side. Growing up in a farmworkers family, I had the first-hand experience that ENSO can impact people’s livelihood. The lack of sub-seasonal to interannual predictability has significant impacts not only in people lives but also in our economy. I hope my research can help improve the combined prediction of the combined impacts of the MJO and ENSO.

My Ph.D. research aims to identify and quantify the multiscale air-sea interaction of the MJO and the El Niño onset with the ultimate goal of predicting El Niño initial development. To understand the complex, multiscale mechanisms of the atmosphere and ocean I am using a structured approach that combines several observational datasets as a baseline to evaluate modeling simulations of MJO and ENSO.

My graduate’s overarching goals are to conduct impactful research and acquire the skills necessary to be an independent research scientist.
